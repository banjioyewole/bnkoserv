I've had the opportunity to work on some interesting projects. Before University, I worked on some software projects for which I had the pleasure of creating their marketing materials. In University I had the opportunity to serve as the design administrator for the student government, and worked to overhaul the brand and market some key events welcoming new and returning students back to campus. Some of us at university formed a company, and I worked to create marketing materials for our products as well. I have collected a sample of designs that I really enjoyed throughout these experiences and share them with you now.

Associated Students of the University of Puget Sound

In 2015 I had the opportunity to work in leadership in the executive branch of the student government. Among many great things we were able to accomplish that year, we also began a rebranding campeign to shift perspective away from ASUPS as a monolyth to something more in line with what it was, an organization operated by the students.

The new brand identity came with creating a new logo, new banner, and introducing stickers, shirts and other goods to get the new face of the student government in front of students. The new ASUPS logo is used as the identifying mark internally and externally so campus visitors and prosective students get a feel of what the students at the university are up to.

LogJam! Campus Welcome Event

Every Fall the University of Puget Sound hosts an all campus welcome event replete with free food, free shows and a festival of clubs and organizations for new and returning students to experience. While returning students know and prepare rigerously to intoruce their club to new students, we market this event very aggressivley. It is the only place to be on that first Friday of fall semester.

The marketing campaign is aided by the university's communications department, but is spearheaded by the Associated Students' Director of Marketing and Outreach, a position I held for Summer and Fall of 2015. While the Director of Marketing and Outreach at ASUPS I was able to work on these posters.
